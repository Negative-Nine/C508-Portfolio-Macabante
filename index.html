Prelim Term Reflection

During the Prelim Term, I was introduced to foundational concepts in NLP that helped me understand the initial stages of working with language data. Starting with NLP Text Preprocessing, I learned how important it is to clean and prepare text data before any analysis can happen. Techniques like tokenization, lemmatization, and removing stop words helped me see how we can shape raw text into something manageable and useful. Without these steps, analyzing the text would be a lot more challenging, as unwanted elements could skew results.

Next, with Implementing Text Representation, I explored ways to turn processed text into formats that algorithms can work with. Methods like Bag-of-Words and TF-IDF were particularly interesting because they showed me how words and phrases can be converted into numerical data. This was my first real experience with text representation, and it felt like a bridge between language and computation.

Altogether, these activities gave me a solid start in NLP. They made me realize how important these early steps are in any NLP project. I now have a clearer picture of how data flows through different stages, and I feel better prepared for more advanced topics in NLP.

Midterm Term Reflection

For the Midterm, I delved into practical applications of NLP, working directly with models and focusing on performance evaluation. Starting with Implementing NLP Application Models using SpaCy, I learned how SpaCy streamlines the process of building various NLP applications. From entity recognition to dependency parsing, using SpaCy’s pre-trained pipelines helped me see how powerful models can simplify complex tasks. This hands-on experience gave me confidence in understanding model functionality and how to adapt it for specific use cases.

Next, Evaluating NLP Model Performance introduced me to key metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score. Learning to assess models was essential, as it showed me how to quantify their effectiveness and pinpoint areas for improvement. By comparing different results, I understood the importance of choosing the right metrics to reflect a model’s real-world performance accurately.

Finally, I worked with a BERT-based QnA System, which was one of the most challenging yet rewarding experiences. Setting up a BERT model for question-answering allowed me to explore deep learning applications in NLP. BERT’s ability to understand context and provide relevant answers highlighted how far NLP has come in recent years. This project gave me a glimpse into cutting-edge technology and sparked my interest in advanced NLP applications.

Overall, these activities expanded my knowledge and appreciation of NLP, as I moved from basic text processing to building and evaluating complex models. I’m now more motivated to explore how these models can be applied to real-world challenges.
